## Introduction to APIs

### What are Are APIs

An APi is a software interface, a building block that works behind the scenes to connect different application and systems so they can share information.

APis are entry points into your organization's capabilities that can be accessed through differnt channels such as browsers, mobile apps, social networks, and IoT devices

Entreprises  can give access to a portal to use their APIs

### Benefits

- **New revenue** Monetize the use of your API
- **Reach and Retention** Understand your customers better through their use of your API
- **Faster partnerships** with well-documented, usable APIs allow partners to onboard themselves in record time example
- **Mobile and Omnichannel** Offering services through APIs is the best way to provide a consistent digital experience accorss all channels
- **Interation analytics** Having customers and partners access services through APIs creates a digital feddback loop, allowing real-time analysis of user behavior
- **Outsource innovation** Publishing a public API can lead to **unanticipated innovation from third party developers
- **Self-service integration** An API-enabled enterprise allows more rapid integration of disparate business capabilities and assembly of new product
- **Organizational agility** Adoption of an "API First"" engineering philosophy along with a microservice architecture maximaizes delivery speed, system stability and scalability
